Switzerland may be small but it has a whole host of great places to visit. There is a wealth of excursions available from pretty lakeside towns and castles to stunning waterfalls, mountains and glaciers.
Among mountain valleys and lakes lie world class international resorts and long list of things to do, including hiking, biking, climbing, paragliding, skiing, kayaking, and tobogganing. Explore this impressive country with our list of the top attractions and best places to visit in Switzerland.


The Matterhorn

This horn-shaped peak is one of the highest mountains the Alps. About 3000 climbers summit the Matterhorn per year. In the summer, up to 150 climbers can attempt an ascent each day. The Swiss-German name for the peak comes from the words Matte meaning ‘meadow’ and horn meaning ‘peak’. It is called Monte Cervino in Italian or Mont Cervin in French. The Matterhorn first appeared on the famous Toblerone packaging in 1960. Before this, an eagle was the main symbol. Have you spotted the bear in the Matterhorn image on Toblerone. This is a reference to Bern, where Theodor Tobler first invented the bar.

Ideas:

  • Gornergrat Railway and Observation Platform
  • Matterhorn Museum
  • Matterhorn Glacier Paradise
  • Rhône Valley

Jungfraujoch: The Top of Europe

The Top of Europe is once of the most iconic and beautiful places located in the Swiss canton of Valais. It overlooks the eternal snows of the Aletsch Glacier from the south side of the Jungfraujoch, at a height of 3,454 metres above sea level. Jungfraujoch is a unique year-round montain destination, It's so much more that the highest train station in Europe. Popular walks include Panorama Way to the sunny, south-facing First slope; the Gletscherschlucht (Glacier Gorge); and Öpfelchüechliwäg, the high-altitude trail from Holenstein to Brandegg through fields of flowers, alpine pastures, and woodlands.You can ride gondolas and cable cars to scenic viewpoints over the Lauterbrunnen Valley.

Ideas:

  • Sphinx Observation Deck & Aletsch Glacier
  • Mönchsjochhütte
  • Ice Palace
  • Alpine Sensation


Interlaken

Interlaken is one of Switzerland most popular summer holiday resorts. Built on a narrow stretch of valley, between the emerald-colored waters of Lake Thun and Lake Brienz, it has old timber houses and parkland on either side of the Aare River. Its surrounding mountains, with dense forests, alpine meadows and glaciers, has numerous hiking and skiing trails. You'll find plenty of things to do in and around Interlaken. Along with the almost endless variety of walks, climbs, and outdoor excursions, there are many other sports available for active travelers. On the right bank of the Aare river is a swimming pool, and visitors will find a golf course in Unterseen, as well as sailing and windsurfing on the lakes, paragliding, horseback riding, and tennis.

Ideas:

  • Harder Kulm
  • Sigriswil Panorama Bridge
  • Giessbach Falls
  • Cruise on Lake Thun or Kayak on Lake Brienz

Lucerne

Lucerne is well known for it's spectacular medieval architecture and stunning location on the edge of Lake Lucerne amid snow capped mountains. The little city punches far above its weight culturally, with its iconic medieval bridge, Kapellbrücke, leaping across the Reuss River, Jean Nouvel-designed KKL arts centre and Sammlung Rosengart, harboring a prized private collection of Picassos. Lucerne is the most populous city in Central Switzerland, and a nexus of economics, transportation, culture, and media in the region.

Ideas:

  • Kapellbrücke (Chapel Bridge) and Old Town
  • Swiss Museum of Transport
  • Explore Lake Lucerne by Boat
  • Mount Pilatus or Mount Rigi

Lake Geneva

Lake Geneva (or Lac Léman) is a crescent-shaped lake shared between France and Switzerland, and overlooked by the Alps. Geneva is one of Switzerland’s most beautiful places. From the international bustle of Geneva to the many vineyards that crawl up the hillsides around the lake, there’s plenty to do in the surrounding region. Windsurfing, water skiing, and kayaking are all possible activities on the lake. Promenades, parks, and gardens surround the lake, and the old town is a lovely spot to stroll among the historic buildings. The Jet d'Eau, a fountain in Lake Geneva shooting water 150 meters into the air, is a famous landmark. Cultural attractions include the Opera House and the Grand Théâtre, which stages international acts.

Ideas:

  • Lake Geneva Cruise Lines
  • Parc Naturel Régional Jura Vaudois
  • Tristan Crist Magic Theatre
  • Lake Geneva Ziplines & Adventure


The Rhine Falls

The Rhine Falls (Rheinfall) at Schaffhausen are the largest falls in Central Europe. Boat trips up the Rhine provide excellent views of the falls, as do the viewing platforms on both sides of the river. Best time to visit is during summer when the mountain snow melts, as part of Switzerland's National Day every August the fall host a spectacular firework display.

Ideas:

  • Boat Cruises & Viewing Platforms
  • Adventure Park
  • Hiking Circular Trail


Zürich

And last but not least, no list of the top things to do in Switzerland would be complete without mentioning Zurich. Zurich is Switzerland's largest city, a major transportation hub, and a top starting point for travelers. A great place to begin a walking tour is the cobbled streets of the Old Town with its quaint shops, cafés, and galleries. Venturing away from the boutiques, visitors will find more than 50 museums and 100 art galleries. From the popular Pride parade and Europe’s largest street rave to the stunning scenery of Lake Zurich there's a lot going on.


Ideas:

  • Lake Zurich
  • Old Town
  • FIFA Museum
  • Lindt Home of Chocolate
